Most leadership support falls into one of three approaches. Understanding the difference matters — because the right one depends on what you actually need.
Approach one
Mentoring.

I had a similar problem once. Here's what I did — and what I'd do differently. Useful when you need a shortcut, or want to learn from someone who's been in a similar position. The risk is that my answer is shaped by my experience, not yours.

Approach two
Consultancy.

Give me the problem. I'll research it, think it through, and come back with a recommendation. Efficient, and useful for specific decisions. But it's my answer — you're implementing someone else's solution, which makes it harder to fully own.

Approach three
Coaching.

I help you find your own answers. It takes more time. But a solution you've reached yourself is one you understand, believe in, and are far more likely to follow through on. In my experience, it's the most effective approach — almost every time.

In practice

My approach draws on all three — because real situations rarely fit neatly into a single mode. Sometimes you need a perspective from someone who's been in a similar position. Sometimes you need someone to do the thinking alongside you and come back with options. And sometimes you need space to work out what you already know but haven't said yet.

But coaching is the backbone. The goal is never to tell you what to do — it's to help you get clearer on what you actually think, what matters most, and what's getting in the way. From there, the path tends to become obvious.

What people bring

There's no typical client. But there are recurring situations — moments where an outside perspective, and someone to think alongside, makes a real difference.

A desire for major change.

Career direction, where you work, how you work. You know something needs to shift — but the size of the change, and the fear of getting it wrong, makes it hard to move.

Thinking about an exit.

You're considering selling a business, stepping back, or moving on. You want to do it well — on your terms, at the right time, for the right reasons.

A life event that changes the equation.

Something has shifted — personally, professionally, or both — and your old way of working no longer fits. You need to work out what does.

Growing a business.

You're building something. The challenges are getting more complex — strategy, people, culture, decisions under pressure. You want a thinking partner who's been in the room.

Team or culture challenges.

Something isn't working in how the team operates — communication, trust, performance, or dynamics. You want to address it properly, not paper over it.

Leadership relationships that aren't working.

Peers, stakeholders, or people above you. The relationship is costing you energy, influence, or results — and you want to change that.

Avoiding burnout. Enjoying work again.

You're effective, but it doesn't feel sustainable. You want to be better with your time, your energy, and your attention — before something breaks.

Stuck. In complexity, or in routine.

The mire of competing demands, unclear priorities, or just the creeping feeling that you're moving but not going anywhere. You need clarity, and someone to help you find it.

How it works

We start with a discovery conversation — not a sales pitch, just an honest look at where you are and where you want to be. From there I'll get a fuller sense of the current situation, what you're trying to achieve, and what's getting in the way.

Sessions are typically 90 minutes, by video call or in person — whichever works. Between sessions, you have access to me for a message or quick call if something comes up and you don't want to wait. Some of the most useful conversations happen in the gaps.

When the problem warrants it, there's the option of a day at the farm in Sussex — proper thinking space, away from the desk and the diary. Fire, fresh air, and enough room to think something through properly.

We begin with a two-session trial. If it's working, we continue. If not, no hard feelings. After the trial, either side can end the engagement with a month's notice.

Frequency
Weekly Suits periods of significant change, pressure, or complexity — when things are moving fast and you need consistent support and challenge close to the action.
Fortnightly The most common rhythm. Enough time between sessions to act, reflect, and bring something new — without losing momentum or thread.
Monthly Works well for people in a more settled phase — using the sessions as a regular space to take stock, course-correct, and think longer-term.
